The best places to visit and live in Norway

Bergen

Bergen is the second largest "city" in Norway. However, it is not the kind of city you are thinking of. It is known for its university. It is surrounded by mountains and water, so it is perfect all year round. You may be thinking it would be unsafe since it is a major city, but the gun death rate in Bergen is 1.75 per 100k residents every year. That means about every 80 days, someone in Bergen will be a victim of gun violence. Bergen is also the perfect vacation destination due to cruise lines that stop here.

Trondheim

Trondheim is known as Norway's capital of knowledge. It has the Norwegian University of Science and Technology which makes this city the leader for innovation. Trondheim is also known for its historic yet colorful town. With boutiques and cafes, it is a picture perfect place. You also will see that there isn't a lot of personal transportation like cars, it is heavy in public transportation and cycling.

Oslo

Oslo is the capital of Norway and also the largest city. If you want to take a little bit of Indy with you to Norway, this is the perfect place to go! With the taller building and more modern feel, Oslo is the home away from home. It houses University of Oslo which is the largest university in Norway. However, Oslo isn't just a concrete jungle, it is also known for is green spaces and surrounding mountains where you get the perfect view of the city. It is a vibrant-social city scene.

Geirangerfjord

Although Geirangerfjord may not be the easiest place to live, it is most certainly a must-see in Norway. It is known for having the steepest cliffs on the west-coast of Norway. The Fjord has basically no shoreline making it feel wider than it actually is. It has the most beautiful waterfalls and sights. If you want to experience the entirety of the Gierangerfjord area, there are numerous hiking trials, and you can even see it from kayaking down the fjord. If you work remote, you may be able to find a cute little cottage in a small town on one of the cliffs.
